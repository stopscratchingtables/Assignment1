A brief history of The Beatles

Beginings in Liverpool, United Kingdom (1957)

The Beatles were formed around the nucleus of members John Lennon and Paul McCartney, who first performed together in Liverpool, England, in 1957. They met each other through the same church they attended.

Please Please Me (1963)

'Please Please Me', was the debut album of The Beatles, produced by George Martin. It was released on EMI's Parlophone label on 22 March 1963. Singles such as 'Please Please Me' and 'Love Me Do' reached number 1 on the NME and Melody maker charts and number 17 on the UK Singles Chart.

Meet the Beatles! (1964)

The Beatles released their second album but this time, it was recorded and released in the United States, issued by Capitol Records. The Beatles also made their first appearance on an American talk show, "The Ed Sullivan Show", hosted by Ed Sullivan. Later that year, They released a follow-up album called 'A'

July 1969

At this point, the beatles were known world-wide with an exponentialy large fanbase. However, the band had gone down a rocky path. With tensions rising among the 4 members, there were reports stating that John Lennon was apparently leaving the band. At this point, the band had released 9 albums, most currently being 'Abbey Road'

The end 1970

Begining of the year, the beatles released their final single as a group, "Hey Jude", which is one of their most popular singles. Alongside, was their final album, "Let it Be" released by Parlaphone studios. However, at the end of the year, Paul McCartney left the beatles and John Lennon decided to file a suit to dissolve the band once and for all.
